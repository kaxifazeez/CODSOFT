A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

"Let us sacrifice our today so that our children can have a better tomorrow." - A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, commonly known as A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist, engineer, and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

Kalam was born in a humble family and faced economic challenges during his childhood.He showed a strong interest in science and mathematics from a young age.He pursued aeronautical engineering and graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1958.

Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and missile programs.He was involved in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and the successful launch of the satellite Rohini-1.He also played a significant role in the development of ballistic missile technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Kalam was the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.He played a crucial role in the successful execution of the tests, which established India as a nuclear weapons state.

Kalam was known for his vision of India becoming a developed nation by the year 2020. He believed in harnessing the power of science and technology to drive India's progress and development. After his tenure as President, Kalam returned to teaching and interacting with students. He often gave motivational speeches to young people, encouraging them to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Kalam was often referred to as the "People's President" due to his approachability and his ability to connect with all sections of society. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, after suffering a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His death was met with an outpouring of grief and tributes from people across India and around the world.

Kalam's legacy lives on through his contributions to science, technology, education, and his inspirational messages. His life and work continue to motivate and inspire countless individuals, particularly the youth, to strive for excellence and contribute to the betterment of society.
